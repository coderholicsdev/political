Professor Yemi Osinbajo

Current Vice President Of The Republic Of Nigeria

Professor Yemi Osinbajo is the 14th and current Vice President of Nigeria and also Senior Advocate of Nigeria(SAN). He was once a Professor of Law at the University of Lagos who aslo served as Attorney General Of Lagos State. Born in Lagos in 1957, Osinbajo is a graduate of the University of Lagos and London School of Economics. Shortly thereafter, he started lecturing at the University of Lagos while practicing law until 1999. After serving as Attorney-General and Commissioner for Justice in the cabinet of Lagos State Governor Bola Tinubu, Osinbajo left government in 2007 and returned to law and lecturing along with preaching in the Redeemed Christian Church of God.

Born in Lagos in 1957, Osinbajo is a graduate of the University of Lagos and London School of Economics. Shortly thereafter, he started lecturing at the University of Lagos while practicing law until 1999. After serving as Attorney-General and Commissioner for Justice in the cabinet of Lagos State Governor Bola Tinubu, Osinbajo left government in 2007 and returned to law and lecturing along with preaching in the Redeemed Christian Church of God.

Osinbajo did his mandatory one-year youth service as a legal officer with Bendel Development and Planning Authority (BDPA) in Bendel State from 1979 to 1980. At the age of 23, Osinbajo began lecturing. He began working as a law lecturer at the University of Lagos in Nigeria in 1981. He was a Senior Lecturer of Law at the University of Lagos from 1983 to 1986. He worked as an Adviser (legal guidance and litigation) to the Attorney-General and Minister of Justice, Bola Ajibola, from 1988 to 1992.  From 1997 to 1999, he was a law professor and the chair of the Department of Public Law at the University of Lagos. From 1999 until 2007, Osinbajo served as a Cabinet Member in the Lagos State Ministry of Justice, as well as Attorney-General and Commissioner for Justice.

Osinbajo was promoted to Senior Partner at Simmons Cooper Partners (Barristers & Solicitors) in Nigeria in 2007. From 2007 to 2013, Osinbajo worked as a Professor of Law at the University of Lagos' Faculty of Law, Department of Public Law. In addition, he was a Senior Lecturer at Lagos State University.

Following the founding of the All Progressives Congress (APC) in 2013, Yemi was charged with designing and producing a manifesto for the new political party alongside other renowned Nigerians. This culminated in the release of the "Roadmap to a New Nigeria," a paper issued by the APC as its manifesto if elected. The Roadmap's highlights included a free school lunch plan and a conditional cash distribution to the 25 million poorest Nigerians if their children are enrolled in school and immunized. There were also other programs aimed at providing economic opportunities for Nigeria's enormous young population.
